INCIDENT LOG — Argo Financial
Date: 2026-05-11
Reported by: Dana Flores, Vendor Risk Management
Incident Commander: Tom Beaumont, Platform Engineering Lead

--- RAW NOTES ---

14:22 — Email received from KYC vendor Veritas ID notifying Argo of a security incident on their platform affecting data shared between 2026-03-01 and 2026-05-10
14:35 — Dana escalated to Tom and Legal immediately
14:50 — Emergency call with Veritas ID. Confirmed ransomware attack on their internal systems. Customer KYC data including government IDs and facial scan data potentially exfiltrated.
15:30 — Argo data scope confirmed with Veritas. 842 Argo customers onboarded during the affected window have data at risk.
16:00 — Decision made to suspend Veritas ID integration immediately. Fallback to manual KYC review for new onboarding.
16:15 — Integration suspended. Engineering team notified.
17:00 — Legal confirmed GDPR notification likely required — KYC data includes EU passport scans.
17:30 — Began drafting customer notification letter.

Data at risk per Veritas ID:
- Full legal names
- Government issued ID numbers (passport, drivers license)
- Facial scan biometric data
- Date of birth
- Address history

Sample affected customers confirmed by Veritas:
- Ahmed Hassan, passport GB-9921044, DOB 1985-07-22, London UK
- Maria Gonzalez, DL CA-8821933, DOB 1991-11-03, San Francisco CA
- Wei Zhang, passport CN-E8821044, facial scan data included

Compliance notes:
- GDPR Article 33 — 72 hour notification clock started at 14:22 today. Deadline: 2026-05-14 14:22
- CCPA — California customers affected, assess notification requirements
- NY SHIELD Act — NY customers in scope
- Biometric data — Illinois BIPA may apply if any IL customers affected
- OCC notification — assess whether this meets threshold for mandatory regulator notification

What went well:
- Veritas notified us within 24 hours of their own discovery
- Integration suspension was fast — under 2 hours from initial notification
- Legal was looped in immediately

What went wrong:
- No contractual requirement for Veritas to maintain SOC 2 Type II — vendor risk gap
- No data minimization — we were sending more customer data to Veritas than required for KYC
- No real-time monitoring of third party data sharing activity

Open questions:
- GDPR clock is running — do we have a DPO assigned to file the Article 33 notification?
- Which IL customers are in the 842 affected — BIPA exposure assessment needed urgently
- Can Veritas confirm whether data was exfiltrated or only encrypted?
- Do we notify all 842 customers now or wait for Veritas forensics to confirm scope?
