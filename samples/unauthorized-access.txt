INCIDENT LOG — Argo Financial
Date: 2026-05-09
Reported by: Priya Nair, Security Operations
Incident Commander: James Wu, CISO

--- RAW NOTES ---

09:02 — SIEM alert triggered. Unusual login pattern detected on internal admin portal.
09:05 — Priya investigated. Single user account (admin_svc_04) logged in from two IPs simultaneously — one internal (10.42.0.18), one external (185.220.101.47 — known Tor exit node)
09:11 — Account admin_svc_04 suspended immediately pending investigation
09:14 — James Wu notified. Legal and Compliance looped in.
09:28 — Forensics started. Reviewed access logs for admin_svc_04 over past 30 days.
09:55 — Confirmed unauthorized access. External session accessed customer data API — /api/v1/customers/search — 47 times between 08:34 and 09:02
10:20 — Scope confirmed. 1,200 customer records potentially exposed including names, email addresses, and partial account numbers.
11:45 — Root cause identified. Service account credentials found hardcoded in a public GitHub repo (argo-internal/data-pipeline) committed by developer Ryan Kowalski (emp ID 10482) on 2026-04-22.
12:00 — GitHub repo made private. Credentials rotated. All active sessions terminated.
12:30 — Incident contained.

Compliance notes:
- GDPR Article 33 — breach notification to supervisory authority required within 72 hours if EU customers affected. Checking customer list now.
- GLBA Safeguards Rule — unauthorized access to nonpublic personal information requires incident response documentation
- PCI-DSS — partial account numbers exposed, assess scope
- State breach notification laws — NY SHIELD Act likely triggered given NY customer presence

What went well:
- SIEM detection was fast — 28 minutes from first unauthorized access to alert
- Containment was clean once scope was confirmed

What went wrong:

- Service account had broader permissions than needed for its role
- No automated alert for access from Tor exit nodes
