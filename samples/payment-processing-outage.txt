INCIDENT LOG — Argo Financial
Date: 2026-05-14
Reported by: Marcus Chen, On-Call Engineer
Incident Commander: Sarah Okonkwo, Platform Lead

--- RAW NOTES ---

02:14 — PagerDuty alert fired. Payment processing service returning 503s. 
02:16 — Marcus paged. Checked Datadog. Error rate at 94% on /api/v2/payments/submit
02:19 — Sarah brought in. Started war room in #incident-p1 Slack channel
02:31 — Identified spike in failed DB connections to payments-db-primary
02:44 — Root cause found. Deployment at 01:52 introduced a connection pool misconfiguration. Max connections set to 5 instead of 500.
02:51 — Rollback initiated by Derek Patel (deploy ID: dpl-882921)
03:04 — Error rate back to baseline. Service confirmed healthy.
03:10 — Post-incident monitoring started. No further anomalies.

Affected customers during window:
- John Ramirez, account #4820194, attempted transfer of $14,500 — failed
- Linda Park, account #9912043, attempted bill payment to Chase — failed  
- Michael Torres, SSN 542-88-1923, flagged for large transaction review — record exposed in error log
- 3,847 additional transactions failed during the 50-minute window

Financial impact:
- Estimated $2.1M in failed transaction volume
- 3 enterprise clients (Meridian Bank, Apex Capital, TrustCore) affected — SLA breach likely for Meridian

Compliance notes:
- Error logs inadvertently captured SSN for Michael Torres and full account numbers for affected users
- Meridian Bank SLA requires 99.95% uptime — we were at 6% for 50 minutes
- Possible PCI-DSS exposure — card-adjacent data in logs
- Legal flagged potential CFPB notification requirement given volume of failed consumer transactions

What went well:
- Detection was fast, under 3 minutes from alert to human eyes
- Rollback was clean, no data loss

What went wrong:
- Deployment went out at 2am without a second reviewer
- Connection pool config was not part of automated pre-deploy checks
- Error logs were not scrubbed before being written to shared logging platform

Open questions:
- Does the Meridian SLA breach trigger a contractual penalty?
- Do we need to notify CFPB given consumer transaction failure volume?
- Who is responsible for adding connection pool validation to deploy checklist?
